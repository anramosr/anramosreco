Research

Working Papers

Trends in Temperature Data: Micro-foundations of Their Nature (with Lola Gadea and Jesús Gonzalo)
Abstract Determining whether Global Average Temperature (GAT) is an integrated process of order 1, I(1), or is a stationary process around a trend function is crucial for detection, attribution, impact and forecasting studies of climate change. In this paper, we investigate the nature of trends in GAT building on the analysis of individual temperature grids. Our 'micro-founded' evidence suggests that GAT is stationary around a non-linear deterministic trend in the form of a linear function with a one-period structural break. This break can be attributed to a combination of individual grid breaks and the standard aggregation method under acceleration in global warming. We illustrate our findings using simulations.
On the Effects of Wildfires on Poverty in Bolivia (with Gustavo Canavire and Alejandro Puerta)
Abstract This paper examines the impact of severe wildfire events on poverty and labor market outcomes in Bolivia. We utilize NASA's MODIS Collection-6 MCD64A1 burned area product to create a panel of burned areas spanning from 2005 to 2020 and merge it with Household Surveys. To attain survey representativeness at a lower geographical level, we aggregate neighboring municipalities using the max-p-region algorithm and evaluate its robustness. We estimate the causal effects of severe wildfire events on poverty, household per-capita income, and the agricultural sector using the interactive fixed effects counterfactual estimator. Our analysis reveals a significant short-term increase in poverty and insignificant effects in the medium term explained by a decline in household per capita and, specifically, agricultural labor incomes in the subsequent years following a wildfire event.
